We are at a turning point.

And this isn't about "improving the tool."
It's about changing our mindset.

## Our Current Goals

**1️⃣ Specialist Self-Service.**
A specialist sends a link to their resume or text in Telegram → we improve it → approve it → send it to the integrator.
First, we test it on ourselves. Then — on actual specialists.

**2️⃣ Agency / Client Personal Account.**
We test it on ourselves.
Next — a pilot with an agency or integrator. Subscription.

**3️⃣ Uploading third-party vacancies from HH and direct outstaffing offers to clients.**
Competitors do this. Nothing prevents us from doing it more technologically.

## The Main Thing — Not the Points

The main thing is the transition:

> From a "tool for ourselves" → to SaaS.

We are no longer just:
- recruiting specialists
- fulfilling requests
- operating as a "smart agency"

We are building:

- AI agents
- self-service
- a scalable model
- a platform, not a service

## Why This Matters

As long as we work "by hand" — growth is linear.
Added people → grew.
Didn't add → stagnated.

SaaS is a different vector.
One agent can process dozens of streams.
One system — to scale without increasing headcount.

It's more complex.
It's slower at the start.
It requires discipline.

But it's the only path to exponential growth.

## What's Important Now

- not to spread ourselves thin
- not to turn everything into custom solutions for a single client
- to build the core product
- to test on ourselves
- to achieve repeatability

We have already proven that we can complete tasks.
Now we need to prove that we can build a system.

## The Most Interesting Stage

Honestly — this is the most interesting stage.
The transition from craft to architecture.

And yes, there will be resistance.
It's always easier to "do it by hand" than to automate correctly.

But if we want to break through the operational ceiling — there's no other way.

Let's move forward 🚀
