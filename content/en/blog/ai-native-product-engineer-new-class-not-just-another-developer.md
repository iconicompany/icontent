🚀 An AI-native product engineer is not "just another developer."
It's a different class.

💡 Look at how classic development works:
Idea → discussion → approval
→ System analyst
→ Detailing
→ Architects (sometimes several)
→ Task assignment
→ Developer only starts after several days

Then:
Development
Testing
Release

And only then do you realize:
👉 Was this feature even necessary?

⌛ A month passes.
💰 The team's budget is consumed.

And you bought... a hypothesis.

❌ The problem is not with people.
The problem is with the model.

You optimize delivery,
but you don't optimize hypothesis testing.

✨ Now, the other side.

AI-native product engineer.

They don't wait for:
- An analyst
- An architect
- Perfect specifications

They:
- Assemble a prototype in a day
- Test a hypothesis
- Look at metrics

⚡ And they do this not once a month.
But several times a week.

📈 The difference is not in "knowing more technologies."
The difference is in behavior:
- Uses AI as a multiplier
- Works from hypotheses
- Thinks in terms of product, not tasks
- Delivers results

🚨 And this is where hiring breaks down.

You keep looking for:
- "5 years of experience"
- "Stack knowledge"

Although the real difference between people is x10.
And it's not about that at all.

⏳ While one team spends a month on one hypothesis,
another tests 10.

🏆 And the winner is not the one with "stronger developers,"
But the one who understands faster what isn't working.

In short:
> 💡 Classic development = optimization of execution
> 🚀 AI-native = optimization of learning

💰 And this completely changes product economics.
