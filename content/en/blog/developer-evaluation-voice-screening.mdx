We've thought long and hard about how to properly assess developers.

And we've come to an uncomfortable conclusion:

- 📄 resumes are noise
- 🧠 interview answers are often prepared
- 🤖 and classic tests are easily bypassed with AI

That's why we're building a different system.

## 🔍 How the New Assessment Will Work

We are moving away from "trusting words" → to skill evidence:

- analysis of code and reactions to errors
- verification of logical thinking, not just the result
- assessment of answer depth in real-time
- several independent AI assessments
- a final calibrated score, not an "interviewer's feeling"

👉 **Key idea:** it's not *what* the candidate said, but *how* they think that matters.

## 🎤 Next Step - Voice Screening

We are currently preparing to launch voice interviews (beta is already internal).

What this will provide:

- live dialogue instead of forms
- skill verification without "prepared answers"
- analysis of terminology, pauses, confidence
- dynamic questions tailored to the candidate
- identification of "hallucinations" and embellished experience

👉 Essentially, this isn't "a bot asking questions," but **AI conducting a technical interview like an engineer**.

## ⚙️ What's Important

This isn't just recruiter automation.

This is an attempt to build a system that:

- learns from hiring outcomes itself
- understands the real skill level, not just the resume
- and over time becomes more accurate than any human
