We've encountered a problem familiar to every recruiter and interviewer: **AI hallucinations in resumes**.

When we ask an LLM to "adapt" a resume for a job opening, it often does it too well. This creates "noise": skills a candidate once heard about magically transform into their core tech stack.

**What does this lead to?**
- ❌ **Data Noise**: Kafka on a resume with no actual experience.
- ❌ **Loss of Trust**: The interviewer uncovers the gap within the first minute, and the entire resume is discarded.
- ❌ **Harm to the Candidate**: They appear worse than they actually are, because it looks like they're lying.

This is "fake precision" at the expense of honest selection.

## 💡 Our New Approach: Verifiable Skill Profiles

We're changing the mechanics. Now we don't "add" skills - we help **verify** them.

### 1. Three Levels of Skill Proficiency (Power of 3)

Instead of a binary "yes/no," we're introducing three clear levels:

✅ **Used in Production** - real commercial experience.
🟡 **Has Related Experience (Analogue)** - worked with similar tools (e.g., RabbitMQ instead of Kafka).
❌ **No Experience** - honest acknowledgment of skill absence.

### 2. Mandatory Verification

No more "quiet" auto-generation. The specialist is **obligated** to explicitly set a level for each discovered or suggested skill.
👉 Cannot be skipped, cannot be left "as is." This eliminates the risk of "embellished" resumes.

### 3. Smart Analogue Suggestion

This is where our AI provides real value. If the system sees that a project requires Kafka, but your profile lists RabbitMQ, it will suggest:
> *"It seems you have experience with queues. Mark Kafka as a related skill?"*

You make the decision yourself and bear the responsibility for it.

### 4. One-Line Proof

For each "related" skill, we ask for a short explanation: *"Kafka - related experience (worked with RabbitMQ in Project X)"*. This is gold for the interviewer: they understand the boundaries of your competencies in advance and can ask the right questions.

## 🔥 What are we actually building?

We're not just making a resume builder. We're building a **verifiable skill profile system**.

This is on par with platforms like Turing or Triplebyte, but enhanced with an AI assistant that helps you be both honest and visible at the same time.
